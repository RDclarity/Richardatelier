Reference — New York, Upper West Side

Upper West Side — a home between two cities.

Full gut renovation of a pre-war apartment near Central Park: new building systems, a reorganized layout, a completely rebuilt bathroom. A human-centered living design project by Richard Atelier — planned between Vienna and New York.

Starting point

A pre-war apartment with a good bones — and a kitchen with no daylight.

The classic enfilade of a 1920s pre-war apartment was still intact, but decades of small interventions had left their mark: a windowless interior kitchen, a bathroom from the nineties, aging systems behind every wall.

Life concept

Not: how do you live in New York? But: how do you live between two cities?

The client, Vienna-born, recently took over the directorship of a Manhattan gallery and still commutes back to Vienna regularly. The goal was never a showroom, but a retreat with the calm of a Viennese apartment — open enough to welcome collectors and artists on short notice, without ever needing to tidy up first.

Spatial feeling

Viennese calm, New York pace.

Warm oak tones and a restrained material palette recall the Vienna apartment of her childhood, while open sightlines and a pass-through kitchen keep pace with New York life. Any room can turn from a quiet reading corner into a space for twenty guests within minutes.

Overall concept

A full gut renovation. Down to the last seam.

From the building systems to the bathroom, nothing was left untouched:

  • Windowless interior kitchen → open kitchen-living area facing the enfilade
  • Nineties-era bathroom → ensuite bath with a natural-stone vanity and rain shower
  • Aging building systems → complete overhaul of electrical, plumbing and HVAC
  • Fragmented room layout → continuous enfilade with double doors
  • Worn floorboards → restored oak flooring in the original pattern
